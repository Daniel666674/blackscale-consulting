Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026

Blackscale Consulting SAS ("Blackscale", "we", "our") is committed to protecting your personal data in compliance with Colombian Law 1581 of 2012 (Personal Data Protection) and Regulatory Decree 1377 of 2013. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and the rights you hold as a data owner (titular).

1.1 Identity of the Responsible Party

The entity responsible for the processing of personal data is Blackscale Consulting SAS, domiciled in Colombia. For inquiries, requests, or complaints regarding your personal data, contact us at hello@blackscale.co.

1.2 Personal Data We Collect

  • Contact information: full name, corporate email address, phone number, and company name submitted through our contact forms or direct communications.
  • Professional information: job title, industry, and business size, used to tailor our services to your needs.
  • Browsing data: IP address, browser type, pages visited, and session duration, collected through cookies and analytics tools (see Cookie Policy).
  • Communications: content of emails, messages, or meeting notes exchanged in the course of a commercial relationship.

1.3 Purpose of Data Processing

Your personal data is collected and processed for the following purposes, as required by Article 13 of Law 1581 of 2012:

  • Responding to inquiries and scheduling strategy consultations.
  • Providing and managing contracted B2B marketing and consulting services.
  • Sending relevant commercial communications, newsletters, or event invitations (with your prior authorization).
  • Fulfilling legal, accounting, and regulatory obligations under Colombian law.
  • Improving our website's performance and user experience through aggregate analytics.

1.4 Your Rights as a Data Owner (Titular)

Under Articles 8 and 21 of Law 1581 of 2012, you are entitled to:

  • Know — Access the personal data we hold about you free of charge.
  • Update & Rectify — Request correction of inaccurate, incomplete, or outdated data.
  • Delete — Request the removal of your data when it is no longer necessary for the declared purpose, subject to legal retention obligations.
  • Revoke Authorization — Withdraw consent for data processing at any time, except where processing is required by law.
  • Complain — File a complaint with the Superintendencia de Industria y Comercio (SIC) if you believe your rights have been violated. SIC contact: www.sic.gov.co.

1.5 How to Exercise Your Rights

To exercise any of the above rights, send a written request to hello@blackscale.co with the subject line "Habeas Data Request". For consultations (consultas), we will respond within ten (10) business days, extendable by five (5) more days with prior notice. For formal complaints (reclamos), we will respond within fifteen (15) business days, extendable by eight (8) more days, as required by Article 14 of Law 1581 of 2012.

1.6 Data Retention

Personal data is retained only for as long as necessary to fulfill the stated purposes or as required by Colombian legal obligations (e.g., accounting records under the Commercial Code — Decreto 410 de 1971). Once the retention period expires, data is securely deleted or anonymized.

1.7 International Data Transfers

In the course of delivering our services, your data may be transferred to third-party service providers located outside Colombia (e.g., CRM platforms, email marketing tools, analytics providers). All such transfers comply with Article 26 of Law 1581 of 2012, which requires that recipient countries provide an adequate level of data protection, or that adequate safeguards (such as contractual clauses) are in place.

1.8 Aviso de Privacidad (Short-Form Notice)

When it is not practical to display this full Privacy Policy at the moment of data collection (e.g., on a contact form), a condensed Aviso de Privacidad is shown at the point of collection, as required by Articles 14 and 15 of Decree 1377 of 2013. This notice identifies Blackscale Consulting as the data controller, states the purpose of the collection, and provides a link to this full policy.

1.9 Policy Updates

This Privacy Policy entered into force on March 1, 2026. Any material changes will be communicated to data subjects through our website or direct notification before the new policy takes effect, as required by Article 13 of Decree 1377 of 2013.

Blackscale does not sell, rent, or trade your personal data to third parties for their own marketing purposes. Data is shared with service providers solely to deliver the services you have requested.

Data Management

Last updated: March 2026

This section describes how Blackscale Consulting manages personal data at an operational level, including security measures, database registration, data minimization practices, and third-party processor relationships.

2.1 Database Registration

In accordance with Article 25 of Law 1581 of 2012 and Decree 090 of 2018, Blackscale Consulting maintains registration of its personal data databases with the National Database Registry (Registro Nacional de Bases de Datos — RNBD) administered by the Superintendencia de Industria y Comercio (SIC), where applicable based on the volume and sensitivity of data processed.

2.2 Security Measures

We implement technical, administrative, and physical safeguards proportionate to the nature and sensitivity of the personal data we process. These include:

  • Encrypted data transmission (TLS/SSL) for all web-based data submissions.
  • Role-based access controls limiting data access to authorized personnel only.
  • Regular security audits and vulnerability assessments of our digital infrastructure.
  • Confidentiality obligations for all employees and contractors who handle personal data.
  • Incident response protocols to detect, contain, and report data breaches in accordance with SIC guidelines.

2.3 Data Minimization

We collect only the personal data that is adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary in relation to the purposes for which it is processed. We do not collect sensitive personal data (as defined by Article 5 of Law 1581 of 2012) such as racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, health data, or biometric information unless strictly necessary and with explicit consent.

2.4 Third-Party Data Processors

We engage third-party service providers who process personal data on our behalf as data processors (Encargados del Tratamiento). These include CRM platforms, email service providers, cloud storage, and analytics tools. Each processor is bound by a data processing agreement that ensures compliance with Law 1581 of 2012 and equivalent protection standards.

2.5 Data Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data security breach that may affect the rights and freedoms of data owners, Blackscale will notify the affected individuals and report the incident to the SIC within the timeframes established by current Colombian regulations. Notification will include the nature of the breach, the data affected, and the remedial measures taken.

Cookie Policy

Last updated: March 2026

This Cookie Policy explains how Blackscale Consulting uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website. In Colombia, the Superintendencia de Industria y Comercio (SIC) has confirmed via Resolución 32.126 de 2022 that cookies collecting personal data are governed by Law 1581 of 2012 in their entirety — including the requirement for prior, express, and informed consent.

4.1 What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They allow the site to remember your preferences and actions over time. We also use similar tracking technologies such as web beacons, pixel tags, and local storage. When these technologies collect information that can identify you directly or indirectly, they are treated as personal data under Law 1581 of 2012.

4.2 Types of Cookies We Use

The SIC classifies cookies used in Colombia into four categories: persistent cookies (remain after the session ends), first-party cookies (set by this site), third-party cookies (set by external services such as Google Analytics or LinkedIn), and supercookies (advanced tracking such as fingerprinting). We use:

  • Session & Functional Cookies: Essential for core website functionality including navigation and form submission. Although Colombian law requires consent for all cookies, these are activated only after you provide consent and refusing them will limit your use of the Site.
  • Analytics Cookies: Help us understand how visitors interact with the Site (pages visited, time spent, traffic source). We use Google Analytics with IP anonymization enabled. Only activated with your prior consent.
  • Marketing & Preference Cookies: Used to deliver relevant content and track the effectiveness of our marketing communications, including the LinkedIn Insight Tag. Only activated with your prior consent.

4.3 Consent Requirements under Colombian Law

Under Resolución SIC 32.126 de 2022, consent for cookies in Colombia must be prior (before any cookie is placed), express (an affirmative action — pre-ticked boxes and implied consent are invalid), and informed (you must know exactly what is being set and why). There is no "strictly necessary" exemption from consent under Colombian law, unlike the GDPR. Our cookie consent banner reflects this: no non-essential cookies are loaded until you actively accept them.

4.4 Your Cookie Choices

On your first visit, our consent banner allows you to accept or reject each cookie category individually. You can update your preferences at any time by clearing cookies in your browser settings or contacting us at hello@blackscale.co. Withdrawing consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal.

4.5 Third-Party Cookies

Third-party services we use (e.g., Google Analytics, LinkedIn Insight Tag) may set their own cookies governed by their respective privacy policies. We only activate these with your consent and configure them to anonymize personal data where technically possible. Links to the privacy policies of key third-party providers are available on request.

4.6 Cookies and Personal Data Rights

Cookie-collected data that identifies or can identify you is personal data under Law 1581 of 2012. You may exercise your full data rights (access, rectification, deletion, revocation of consent) in relation to this data by contacting us at hello@blackscale.co with the subject line "Cookie Data Request".

GDPR · EU Regulation 2016/679 · Ley 1581 de 2012

GDPR Compliance

Last updated: March 2026

Applicable when serving EU/EEA-based clients or processing EU resident data

Although Blackscale Consulting is incorporated and operates in Colombia, the European Union's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR — Regulation 2016/679) may apply to our processing activities when we offer services to clients or contacts located in the European Union or European Economic Area (EEA), pursuant to GDPR Article 3(2) (extra-territorial scope).

5.1 Colombia's Adequacy Status

Colombia does not hold an EU adequacy decision under GDPR Article 45. The European Commission's current list of adequate third countries does not include Colombia. This means that personal data transfers from the EU/EEA to Blackscale in Colombia require a valid legal safeguard under GDPR Article 46.

We address this through Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) — the EU Commission-approved contract templates updated in June 2021 — incorporated into data processing agreements with EU clients upon request. In December 2025, Colombia's SIC also issued Circular Externa No. 003 de 2025 introducing voluntary Colombian model contractual clauses for international data transfers, aligned with GDPR-style transfer mechanisms. We support the use of either framework depending on the applicable law.

5.2 Legal Basis for Processing EU Resident Data

When processing personal data of EU/EEA residents, we rely on one or more of the following legal bases under GDPR Article 6:

  • Contract: Processing is necessary for the performance of a service agreement with you or your organization (Art. 6(1)(b)).
  • Legitimate Interest: Processing is necessary for our legitimate business interests (e.g., marketing to existing clients), provided your interests do not override ours (Art. 6(1)(f)).
  • Consent: For non-essential cookies, newsletter subscriptions, and marketing communications, we rely on your explicit prior consent (Art. 6(1)(a)).
  • Legal Obligation: Where processing is required to comply with applicable law (Art. 6(1)(c)).

5.3 Rights of EU/EEA Data Subjects

In addition to the rights under Colombian law (Section 1.4), EU/EEA residents are entitled to the following GDPR rights:

  • Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten") — Art. 17: Request deletion of your data where there is no compelling reason for continued processing.
  • Right to Restriction of Processing — Art. 18: Request that we limit how we use your data under certain circumstances.
  • Right to Data Portability — Art. 20: Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format and transfer it to another controller.
  • Right to Object — Art. 21: Object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes.
  • Right not to be subject to automated decision-making — Art. 22: We do not make decisions with significant legal effects based solely on automated processing.

5.4 Data Protection Officer

Blackscale Consulting does not meet the thresholds requiring a mandatory Data Protection Officer under GDPR Article 37. However, all data protection inquiries, including requests from EU/EEA data subjects, are handled directly by our founding team. Contact: hello@blackscale.co.

5.5 Supervisory Authority

If you are located in the EU/EEA and believe we have not complied with GDPR, you have the right to lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority. A list of EU data protection authorities is available at the European Data Protection Board website.

Our primary data protection framework is Colombia's Ley 1581 de 2012. GDPR obligations apply in addition to Colombian law whenever we process data of EU/EEA residents, and we treat both frameworks as complementary rather than in conflict.

Ley 1618 de 2013 · NTC 5854 · WCAG 2.1

Accessibility Statement

Last updated: March 2026

Blackscale Consulting is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for people with disabilities. We believe that all users — regardless of ability — deserve equal access to our content and services. We continuously improve the user experience and apply relevant accessibility standards.

6.1 Legal Framework

Our accessibility commitment is grounded in the following Colombian and international standards:

  • Ley Estatutaria 1618 de 2013 — Guarantees the full exercise of rights by persons with disabilities in Colombia, implementing the UN Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities. Article 16, numeral 11 specifically requires public and private entities to adopt web accessibility standards guaranteeing full access for persons with sensory disabilities.
  • NTC 5854 — The Colombian Technical Standard for Web Accessibility, ratified by ICONTEC on June 15, 2011, based on WCAG 2.0. It defines three conformance levels (A, AA, AAA), with Level AA being the standard required for compliance purposes in Colombia.
  • Resolución MinTIC 1519 de 2020 — Updated the operative Colombian accessibility standard to align with WCAG 2.1, requiring Level AA conformance. Mandatory for government entities and private entities providing public services or operating under government contracts; strongly advisable for all private digital services under Ley 1618.
  • WCAG 2.1 (Level AA) — Our target compliance level, consistent with both Resolución 1519 de 2020 and international best practice.

6.2 Our Accessibility Measures

  • Semantic HTML structure with proper heading hierarchy (H1–H4) to support screen reader navigation.
  • All images and icons include descriptive alt attributes or aria-label values.
  • Color contrast ratios meet or exceed WCAG 2.1 AA minimums (4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text).
  • All interactive elements (links, buttons, form fields) are fully keyboard-navigable with visible focus indicators.
  • Form fields are associated with descriptive labels and include error messages that are accessible to screen readers.
  • Font sizes are defined in relative units (rem/em) to support browser zoom and user font preferences.
  • The website is tested for compatibility with common assistive technologies including NVDA, JAWS, and VoiceOver.

6.3 Known Limitations

While we strive for full WCAG 2.1 AA compliance, some content or features may not yet fully meet all criteria. We are actively working to address these gaps. If you encounter an accessibility barrier, please let us know.

6.4 Feedback and Accessibility Requests

If you experience difficulty accessing any content on our website or require the information in an alternative format (e.g., large print, plain text, or audio description), please contact us:

  • Email: hello@blackscale.co
  • Subject line: "Accessibility Request"

We will respond within five (5) business days and make reasonable efforts to accommodate your request or provide an accessible alternative.

This is a living document. As we continue to develop and improve the Blackscale website, we will periodically review and update our accessibility practices to reflect advancements in standards and technology.
